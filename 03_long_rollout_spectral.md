# Long-Horizon Stability & Spectral Analysis

This document details the spectral properties of the learned continuous generator and evaluates its stability over extreme rollout horizons, addressing concerns regarding the "closure problem" and autoregressive drift.

### 1. Spectral Bias & The Closure Problem
**Addressed to:** Reviewer Ge7F (Finite-dimensional linear truncation of chaotic features)

**Experiment:** We performed a Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) analysis on the spatial and temporal domains of the generated flow fields and computed the eigenvalue spectrum of the learned continuous generator $\mathbf{K}_{\text{cont}}(\phi)$.

**Observations:**
* **Physical Low-Pass Filter:** The finite-dimensional Koopman projection acts as a spatial low-pass filter. While the diffusion baseline (ACDM) synthesizes fine-scale textures, it risks phase drift. The KAE smooths out high-frequency turbulent textures but accurately locks onto dominant macro-scale vortex shedding frequencies (Figure 1).
* **Asymptotic Dissipativity:** The eigenvalue spectrum of the learned operator lies almost entirely in the left half of the complex plane ($Re(\lambda) < 0$) (Figure 2). This ensures that numerical integration errors and high-frequency stochastic noise naturally decay, rather than compounding over time.

### 2. Extreme 1000-Step Rollout Stability
**Addressed to:** All Reviewers (Long-horizon forecasting boundaries)

**Experiment:** We subjected the models (trained on $N=8$ steps) to a 1000-step autoregressive rollout in the highly chaotic Transonic regime to test their ultimate failure modes.

**Observations:**
* **Unconstrained Divergence (ACDM):** The diffusion baseline prioritizes step-to-step texture by injecting stochasticity. Over long horizons, these microscopic errors compound exponentially. Spatial correlation drops to zero, and the physical structure collapses into numerical noise (Figures 3 & 4).
* **Limit Cycle Stability (Continuous KAE):** Constrained by its strictly linear, dissipative latent dynamics, the KAE's errors cannot compound to infinity. Instead of diverging, the predictions gracefully degrade into a stable, physically consistent limit cycle (the fundamental Karman vortex shedding base flow). $L_2$ error remains strictly bounded.

### 3. Zero-Shot Temporal Generalization & Internal Consistency
**Addressed to:** Reviewer RCnK (Internal consistency of continuous formulation)

**Experiment:** We compared trajectories generated by step-by-step numerical integration (RK4) against the exact closed-form analytical solution $z(\tau) = \exp(\mathbf{K}_{\text{cont}}\tau)z_0$. We also queried the model at fractional, untrained time horizons ($\Delta t=0.05s$ and $\Delta t=0.20s$) compared to its $\Delta t=0.10s$ training baseline.

**Observations:**
* **Analytical Consistency:** The numerical integration perfectly aligns with the exact analytical matrix exponentiation (Figure 6), confirming the fast-forwarding inference is mathematically sound.
* **Temporal Interpolation:** Because dynamics are learned in continuous time, evaluating the model zero-shot at finer or coarser time steps yields perfectly aligned flow fields at corresponding physical timestamps (Figure 5). Standard discrete models cannot perform this without retraining.

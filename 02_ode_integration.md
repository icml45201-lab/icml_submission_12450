# ODE Solvers & Temporal Generalization

This document details the evaluation of the learned continuous-time generator ($\mathbf{K}_{\text{cont}}$) across different numerical integrators and step sizes, addressing requests for adaptive solver testing and irregular temporal sampling.

### 1. Adaptive Solvers & Extreme Step Sizes
**Addressed to:** Reviewer B4CM (Adaptive-step solvers, irregular sampling)

**Experiment:** We evaluated the continuous-time KAE's latent ODE using multiple numerical integrators—including the adaptive-step `Dopri5`—across integration step sizes ranging from $\Delta t$ = 0.05s up to $\Delta t$ = 1.00s (a 10x extrapolation beyond the training resolution).

**Observations (Table 1):**
* **Solver Parity at Small Steps:** At step sizes $\Delta t$ < 0.15s, all solvers, including the adaptive `Dopri5`, perform comparably to `RK4`. 
* **Physical Stiffness:** At larger step sizes ($\Delta t$ > 0.50$s), explicit first-order solvers like `Euler` and `Midpoint` diverge numerically ($> 10^{13}$ error). This occurs because the Koopman operator accurately captures dissipative, high-frequency fluid modes, resulting in a mathematically "stiff" ODE.
* **Higher-Order Stability:** Higher-order methods (`RK4`, `Dopri5`) possess stability regions large enough to encompass these stiff dissipative eigenvalues, maintaining bounded physical stability even at a $1.00$s jump step.

### 2. Analytical Integration & Zero-Shot Generalization
**Addressed to:** Reviewers B4CM (Irregular sampling), z2Gs (Internal consistency of analytical vs. numerical integration)

**Experiment:** We compared the trajectories generated by standard numerical integration (`RK4`) against the exact analytical solution computed via the matrix exponential: $z(\tau) = \exp(\mathbf{K}_{\text{cont}}\tau)z_0$.

**Observations:**
* **Internal Consistency:** The numerical integration (`RK4`) closely matches the exact analytical matrix exponentiation (Figure 1). 
* **Irregular Sampling:** Because the operator is learned in continuous time, the model can perform zero-shot evaluation at unseen, irregular temporal resolutions without retraining.

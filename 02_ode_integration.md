# Extreme ODE Solver Stress-Testing & Temporal Generalization

**Reviewer Comments Addressed:**
* **B4CM**: Evaluate adaptive-step ODE solvers like Dopri5. Demonstrate value for irregularly sampled temporal data.
* **z2Gs**: Internal consistency of analytical vs. numerical integration?

**Response & Empirical Evidence:**

**1. Adaptive Solvers & Numerical Stiffness (Addressing B4CM)**
* **Experiment**: We evaluated the continuous latent generator across 7 integrators (including Dopri5 and Euler) at varying step sizes up to $\Delta t = 1.00s$.
* **Result**: At extreme step sizes ($\Delta t \ge 0.50s$), explicit Euler diverges due to the stiffness of the learned dissipative eigenvalues. Adaptive `Dopri5` and standard `RK4` successfully bound the errors and maintain stability. 

**Table: Integration Step Size Stress Test (MSE $\times 10^{-3}$)**
| Step ($\Delta t$) | Solver | $Tra_{ext}$ | $Tra_{long}$ (240 steps) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0.05 s** | RK4 / Dopri5 | 1.9 ± 1.1 | 14.6 ± 0.9 |
| | Euler | 1.8 ± 1.1 | 18.8 ± 1.3 |
| **0.50 s** | Dopri5 | 5.0 ± 1.3 | 14.8 ± 1.0 |
| | Euler | 19.1 ± 0.8 | $4.88 \times 10^{14}$ (Diverged) |
| **1.00 s** | **RK4** | **6.0 ± 0.5** | **15.1 ± 2.0** |
| | **Dopri5** | **8.5 ± 1.1** | **14.7 ± 1.4** |

**2. Zero-Shot Temporal Super-Resolution & Analytical Consistency (Addressing B4CM, z2Gs)**
* **Experiment**: We evaluated the model zero-shot at unseen fractional time horizons ($\Delta t=0.05s$ and $\Delta t=0.20s$) using both numerical integration and exact matrix exponentiation $z(\tau) = \exp(\mathbf{K}_{\text{cont}}\tau)z_0$.
* **Result**: [See Figure: delta_t_comparison.png]. Trajectories generated by RK4 perfectly align with the exact analytical solution across different step sizes without retraining, confirming a time-invariant continuous generator.
